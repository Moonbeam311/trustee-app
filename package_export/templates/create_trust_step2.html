Create Trust Wizard — Step 2: Trust Type and Purpose

What is this?
This step defines the general structure and intended use of the trust.
Why this matters
Your trust type and purpose affect how the trust is administered, what follow-up documents are recommended, and how later steps in the system will behave.
What do these mean?
Revocable: usually more flexible and easier to modify.
Irrevocable: usually more structured and harder to change once established.
98 Trust: advanced custom trust mode for expanded structure and administration.
Why are we asking this?
The trust purpose helps determine what records, assets, and administration steps are likely to matter most later in the process.
What is this?
Cash Method: records income when received and expenses when paid.
Accrual Method: records income when earned and expenses when incurred.
What is this?
Private Office Mode: internal trust setup, administration, and drafting workflows.
Public Filing / External Mode: workflows intended to support outward-facing or filing-related records.
