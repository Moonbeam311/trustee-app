Create Trust Wizard — Step 3: Parties and Roles

What is this?
This step identifies the key people connected to the trust.
Why this matters
The trust cannot be structured clearly without identifying who creates it, who manages it, who may step in later, and who benefits from it.
What is this?
The settlor or grantor is the person who creates the trust and places property or authority into it.
What is this?
The trustee is the person responsible for administering the trust and acting according to its terms.
What is this?
A successor trustee is the person who may step in if the current trustee dies, resigns, or becomes unable to act.
What is this?
A beneficiary is the person or party for whose benefit the trust is created or administered.
