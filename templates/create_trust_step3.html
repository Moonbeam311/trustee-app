Create Trust Wizard — Step 4 of 8: Trustee and Beneficiary Structure

What is this?
This step defines who will manage the trust and who will benefit from it.
Why this matters
A trust requires clearly identified fiduciaries and beneficiaries to function properly. This step establishes operational control and beneficial interest.
The trustee is responsible for administering the trust and acting in accordance with its terms and fiduciary duties.
The successor trustee may step in if the current trustee is unable or unwilling to serve.
The beneficiary is the person or entity for whose benefit the trust is administered.
